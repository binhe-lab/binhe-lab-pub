Evolution of Stress Response

Stress response is essential for survival. However, as species evolve and occupy novel ecological niches, they may encounter new patterns of stresses, for which their inherited stress response network may no longer be optimal. How do stress response networks evolve in the face of novel challenges? Our lab focuses on a group of single cellular yeast, which contains both free-living and commensal species. Our goal is to 1) reveal general rules about gene regulatory network (GRN) evolution and 2) identify how microbes adapt to the host environment, which may inform treatment and disease prevention.
